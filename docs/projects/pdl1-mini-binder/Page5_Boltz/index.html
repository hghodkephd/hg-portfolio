What is Boltz-2 and why does it matter?

So far in this project, every structural analysis has relied on geometry that originated from RFdiffusion. The backbone was designed by RFdiffusion, the sequence was designed by ProteinMPNN on that backbone, ESMFold confirmed the sequence folds into that backbone shape, and the fingerprinting and MD analyses all started from the RFdiffusion-designed complex pose. Every step carried forward the structural assumptions of the first step.

That raises a question: if the binder sequence folds correctly on its own (passing the ESMFold check), does it also form a complex with PD-L1? Single-chain fold recapitulation does not guarantee multi-chain interaction.

Boltz-2 is an open-source deep-learning model from MIT (Wohlwend et al., 2024–2025) that predicts the 3D structure of biomolecular complexes from sequence alone, achieving AlphaFold 3-level accuracy. We give it two protein sequences — binder and PD-L1 — with no structural hints. It predicts how they fold together and reports confidence metrics, particularly the interface predicted TM-score (ipTM), which has been shown to correlate with experimental binding success better than physics-based scores like Rosetta energies.

What Boltz-2 is NOT used for here: Boltz-2 includes a binding affinity prediction module, but this was trained on protein–small molecule data, not protein–protein interactions. A recent study (King et al., 2025) showed that Boltz-2's affinity head underperforms sequence-based alternatives for protein–protein affinity. We extract only the structure confidence metrics, which are validated for protein–protein complexes.

Results

28 of 31 designs validated. Boltz-2 independently confirms plausible binder–PD-L1 interfaces for 90% of our designs. Zero designs failed outright. Three designs fell in the uncertain zone (ipTM 0.20–0.41). The top-scoring design achieved ipTM = 0.963 — exceptionally high confidence.

Confidence by hotspot configuration

Cluster A designs scored highest on average (mean ipTM 0.821), followed by Cluster B (0.772) and Distributed (0.667). This is consistent with a plausible structural interpretation: concentrated hotspot clusters give Boltz a clearer docking signal because the binder has a well-defined binding patch, while distributed hotspots spread the interface thinner and make the docking geometry less deterministic for the prediction model.

Confidence by binder length

The 25-mer and 70-mer designs showed similar mean ipTM (~0.80), while 100-mers had the widest variance (min 0.199, max 0.941). Longer binders have more possible docking orientations, which may make Boltz's prediction task harder — but the best 100-mers scored nearly as high as the best 70-mers.

Boltz confidence is independent of prior metrics

An important finding: ipTM shows essentially zero correlation with ESMFold alignment RMSD (Spearman ρ = +0.004) and no significant correlation with ProteinMPNN scores. This means Boltz is measuring something genuinely independent from what the upstream pipeline measured — exactly what we want from an orthogonal validation. If ipTM simply tracked pLDDT, it wouldn't add information.

What this means for the project

Boltz-2 validation answers the specific question this page set out to ask: is the binding interface encoded in the designed sequence, or only in the designed structure? For 28 of 31 designs, the answer is yes — an independent structure prediction model, given only sequences, recovers a plausible binder–target complex.

This result has practical consequences for the next step. Affinity scoring (Part 6) will prioritize the 28 Boltz-validated designs using PRODIGY empirical scoring, energy minimization, and extended MD simulation. The three uncertain designs will be included for completeness but deprioritized in the construct selection panel.

Caveats

Boltz-2 confidence scores are not experimental binding affinities. A high ipTM indicates that the prediction model believes the interface is real — it does not prove that the binder will bind in vitro. There is also a known lack of agreement between Boltz-2 ipTM scores and AlphaFold 3 ipTM scores for the same interactions (UCSF, 2025), meaning the absolute values should be treated as one model's opinion, not ground truth. The scores are most useful for relative ranking within a campaign, not for absolute binding prediction.
